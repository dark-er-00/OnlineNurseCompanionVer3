First Aid Guide

Quick emergency care instructions for common situations

Call Emergency Services Immediately If:

  • Person is unconscious
  • Not breathing
  • Heavy bleeding that won't stop
  • Chest pain with difficulty breathing
  • Seizure longer than 5 minutes
  • Apply firm pressure using clean cloth or gauze.
  • Keep pressure for several minutes without checking repeatedly.
  • Elevate the injured area if possible.
  • Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop after 10 minutes.
  • Cool burn under running water for 10–20 minutes.
  • Do NOT apply ice directly.
  • Cover loosely with sterile dressing.
  • Seek help for large, blistering, or facial burns.
  • Follow R.I.C.E: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation.
  • Apply ice 15–20 minutes every hour.
  • Avoid putting weight on the injured area.
  • Seek medical care if swelling is severe.
  • Ask “Are you choking?” if the person can respond.
  • Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Call emergency services if the person becomes unconscious.
