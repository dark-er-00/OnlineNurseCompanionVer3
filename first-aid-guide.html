Emergency Topics

Call Emergency Services Immediately If:

  • Person is unconscious
  • Not breathing
  • Heavy bleeding that won’t stop
  • Chest pain with difficulty breathing
  • Seizure longer than 5 minutes

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

Used when a person is unresponsive and not breathing.

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest.
  3. Place the other hand on top and interlock fingers.
  4. Push hard and fast (100–120 compressions per minute).
  5. Allow full chest recoil between compressions.
  6. If trained, give 2 rescue breaths after 30 compressions.

Continue until help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)

  1. Ask if the person can speak or cough.
  2. Stand behind them and wrap arms around waist.
  3. Make a fist above the navel.
  4. Perform quick upward abdominal thrusts.
  5. Repeat until object is expelled.

If the person becomes unconscious, begin CPR.

Severe Bleeding

  1. Apply firm, direct pressure using cloth or bandage.
  2. Maintain pressure continuously.
  3. Elevate injured area if possible.
  4. Do not remove large embedded objects.

Burns

  1. Cool burn under clean running water for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove tight jewelry.
  3. Cover loosely with sterile dressing.
  4. Do NOT apply ice, oil, or toothpaste.

Seizures

  1. Move harmful objects away.
  2. Place something soft under head.
  3. Do not restrain the person.
  4. Do not put anything in the mouth.
  5. Time the seizure.

Fainting

  1. Lay the person flat on their back.
  2. Elevate legs slightly.
  3. Loosen tight clothing.
  4. Ensure proper ventilation.
